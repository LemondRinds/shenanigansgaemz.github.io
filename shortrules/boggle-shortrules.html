Boggle short rules

To play

  1. Shake the grid of cubes and place it on the table. Remove the lid and push unslotted cubes into holes.
  2. For 4 minutes: each player makes a list of 4+ letter words spelled from continuous strings of cubes on the grid, connected adjacently or diagonally, using cubes once max per word.
  3. Players recite their lists in turn. Cross out non-Scrabble approved words and all words on more than one list.
  4. Remaining words are worth their length minus 3 points, except affixes which are always worth 1.
  5. Repeat until satisfied.

Affixes

Words made by tacking common prefixes or suffixes to a base word that don't form a new word with a distinct meaning are affixes.

  • "Findings" is find+ing+s, and so is an affix. It's worth 1 point.
  • "Submarine" is sub+marine but it means something distinct from sub+marine, and so is worth the full (9 - 3 =) 6 points.

Common prefixes: de, dis, mis, out, over, pre, re, sub, un, under

Common suffixes: s, es, ed, ied, er, ier, ing, ly, y
